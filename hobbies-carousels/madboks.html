Madboks crates

These green crates are the reason of the organization name. Supermarkets collect in these crates the products that they cannot sell anymore but is still perfectly good to eat.

Collection shift

I volunteer to pick up these crates from different supermarkets around the city with a van and deliver them to the headquarter, where they are sorted and distributed to people who sign up for the service. In this picture you can see the van loaded to the brim with crates. One van can carry between 200 and 300 kgs of food, and considering that we use two or three vans per day, and we collect three times per week, we save around 1.5 ton of food every week.

Sorting process

The sorting process is crucial to ensure that the food items are properly categorized and distributed. Volunteers work together to check the quality of the items, sort them into different categories, and pack them back in crates for distribution.
